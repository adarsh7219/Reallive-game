Know Your Family

How income and wealth are used in RealLives to determine family's economic status:

Five different economic status categories are available on RealLives: Bottom 10%, Barely Managed, Middle Income, Rich, and Super Rich.

Depending on the household's income and/or wealth, it is assigned to one of the above categories. The household's economic status will depend on which of these two economic statuses is the highest.

In its early years, a household might have an income, but some savings and therefore little wealth. Based on income, such a household's economic status will be 'Middle Income' and based on wealth, it may fall into the 'bottom 10%' category. Therefore, this household has a middle-income status.

With age, household income will be zero since earning members have retired, but the household has enough savings and investments (wealth). The economic status of such households is 'Bottom 10%' based on their income. This is because no one is earning and economic status based on wealth could be 'Middle income' or 'Rich' or even 'Super Rich'. Therefore, the economic status of this household will be 'Middle Income' or 'Rich' or 'Super Rich'.
